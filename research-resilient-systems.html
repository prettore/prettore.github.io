Resilient Systems for Smart Environments

In: Resilient Systems

Advancements in communication and processing technologies have fostered the emergence and interaction of paradigms that significantly contribute to the socio-economic and technological development of modern societies. Noteworthy paradigms include the Internet of Things, Big Data, Cloud Computing, and Artificial Intelligence.

These paradigms intersect to create innovative models in various domains, such as Smart Cities, Intelligent Transportation Systems, Industry 4.0, Smart farming, and Modern Warfare. However, these models also present several challenges. From a data perspective, issues like the lack of standardization, quality, reliability, and limited spatiotemporal coverage are prevalent. From a systems perspective, there is a need for solutions that can adapt to increasingly dynamic scenarios.

This project seeks to comprehensively investigate these challenges and implement solutions to enhance data quality and develop context-aware systems that can adapt to change. The underlying hypothesis is that applications and models that integrate heterogeneous context data are more robust and accurate in monitoring, predicting, and analyzing. These systems are also more resilient to environmental changes, allowing for more informed decision-making.
